# IDEA Analysis - Federal Special Education Law
**Date:** March 26, 2026  
**Compiled by:** Arthur

---

## Overview

IDEA (Individuals with Disabilities Education Act) is the federal law governing special education for children ages 3-22. It is codified at **34 CFR Part 300**.

---

## Key Definitions

### Child with a Disability (300.8)
A child with a disability includes:
- Intellectual disability
- Hearing impairment (including deafness)
- Speech or language impairment
- Visual impairment (including blindness)
- Serious emotional disturbance
- Orthopedic impairment
- Autism
- Traumatic brain injury
- Other health impairment
- Specific learning disability
- Deaf-blindness
- Multiple disabilities

**Age range:** 3 through 21 (some states include infants/toddlers under Part C)

---

## Core Entitlement: FAPE

### Free Appropriate Public Education (300.17)

FAPE means special education and related services that:
1. **Are provided at public expense** — free to the family
2. **Meet state standards** — including all IDEA requirements
3. **Include appropriate preschool through secondary education**
4. **Are provided in conformity with an IEP** that meets requirements

**Key insight:** "Appropriate" does NOT mean "best" — but it must be designed to meet the child's unique needs and provide educational benefit.

---

## Individualized Education Program (IEP)

### What an IEP Must Include (per §300.320):
- Current performance statements
- Annual goals
- Special education and related services
- Supplementary aids and services
- Program modifications
- Testing accommodations
- Placement decisions
- Transition services (by age 16)
- Communication needs

---

## Least Restrictive Environment (LRE)

### Key Principle (300.114-300.120):
- Children with disabilities must be educated with children who are not disabled **to the maximum extent appropriate**
- Removal from regular education occurs only when the nature or severity of the disability is such that education in regular classes with supplementary aids and services cannot be achieved satisfactorily

---

## Procedural Safeguards (300.500+)

Parents have the right to:
- Be informed about and consent to evaluations
- Participate in IEP meetings
- Review all records
- Request due process hearings
- File state complaints
- Mediation
- Attorney fees (in some cases)

## Key Parts of IDEA

| Part | What It Covers |
|------|----------------|
| **Subpart A** | General provisions, definitions |
| **Subpart B** | State eligibility, FAPE requirements |
| **Subpart C** | Preschool programs (Part B) |
| **Subpart D** | Federal Grants |
| **Subpart E** | Procedural safeguards |
| **Subpart F** | Miscellaneous |
