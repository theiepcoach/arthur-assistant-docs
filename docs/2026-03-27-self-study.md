## COMPLEX QUESTION 1: FAPE Standard Evolution

**Question:** How has the FAPE standard evolved from Rowley (1982) to Endrew F. (2017), and what does "appropriately ambitious" actually mean in practice?

**My Answer:**
- **Rowley (1982):** Two-prong test. Procedural compliance + "reasonably calculated to enable child to achieve passing marks and advance from grade to grade." Did NOT require states to maximize potential.
- **Endrew F. (2017):** Superseded Rowley's substantive prong. Now requires IEP be "reasonably calculated to enable a child to make progress appropriate in light of the child's circumstances." Every child should have chance to meet "challenging objectives."
- **"Appropriately ambitious":** Means individualized - progress appropriate for THAT child's circumstances, not just "merely more than de minimis."

**Gap Check:** Need to verify if courts actually apply this consistently. Zirkel analysis shows 79% district-favorable rulings, suggesting Endrew F. has "negligible impact" in practice.

## COMPLEX QUESTION 2: RTI vs PSW Methods

**Question:** A student has been in RTI for 6 months with Tier 2 reading intervention but shows minimal progress. The school wants to continue RTI before evaluating for special education. What are the parent's rights?

**My Answer:**
- **OSEP 11-07 is clear:** RTI is a tool for identification/early intervention, NOT a substitute for evaluation
- School CANNOT use RTI to delay or deny evaluation
- Parent can request evaluation at ANY time
- The student's lack of response to intervention is actually DATA that supports eligibility
- The 60-day evaluation timeline starts once parent consents

**Legal Support:** 34 CFR §300.309 states RTI can be used as component but cannot delay evaluation.

## COMPLEX QUESTION 3: Texas vs Kentucky Procedural Differences

**Question:** A parent moves from Texas to Kentucky mid-school-year. What procedural differences must the new school know about?

**My Answer:**
| Issue | Texas | Kentucky |
|-------|-------|----------|
| PWN Notice | 5 school days | "Reasonable time" |
| ARC Notice | 7 days (5 days for discipline) | 7 days (24 hrs for safety) |
| Evaluation Timeline | 45 school days + 30 calendar days for ARD | 60 school days |
| Class Size - SLD Primary | 15 | 10 |
| Surrogate Assignment | 30 days | 30 days |

**Key Point:** Both states enhance federal baseline, so both are valid. Parent retains all federal rights.

## COMPLEX QUESTION 4: Manifestation Determination Complexity

**Question:** A student with ADHD receives a 10-day suspension for disruptive behavior. During the manifestation determination, the team discovers the student's behavior was partly due to not receiving their medication at school. What happens?

**My Answer:**
- This is a **disability-based manifestation issue**
- If medication is a related service/FAPE component, denial could be denial of FAPE
- Team should:
  1. Determine if behavior is manifestation of disability
  2. Consider if lack of medication provision caused/worsened behavior
  3. May need to reconvene to address medication administration
- The student may need to return to prior placement while this is resolved
- School cannot discipline for behaviors caused by failure to provide FAPE

**Legal Support:** IDEA §300.530(d)(1)(i) - if manifestation, return to prior placement unless school and parent agree otherwise.

## COMPLEX QUESTION 5: The Dyslexia-IDEA Connection

**Question:** A Texas student is identified as "at risk" for dyslexia on universal screening. The school says they'll provide dyslexia intervention but doesn't want to evaluate for special education. What are the parent's options?

**My Answer:**
- Screening ≠ Evaluation
- Texas law (TEC §38.003) requires dyslexia screening, but screening is NOT an IDEA evaluation
- Parent can request FIIE (Full Individual Initial Evaluation) at ANY time
- If student has dyslexia, they likely qualify under SLD (Specific Learning Disability) category
- **Dyslexia = SLD** under IDEA - affects basic reading skills, reading fluency
- The school MUST evaluate if parent requests, within 45 school days of consent
- Parent can file state complaint if school refuses/delays
- Section 504 provides accommodations even if not IDEA-eligible

**Key Timeline:** 45 school days from consent for FIIE in Texas.

## COMPLEX QUESTION 6: Compensatory Education After Endrew F

**Question:** A parent removes their child from public school after the school denied appropriate services for 2 years. The child was making "merely de minimis" progress. What remedies are available?

**My Answer:**
- **Endrew F. rejected "merely de minimis" as insufficient for FAPE**
- Parent may be entitled to:
  1. **Compensatory education:** Additional services to make up for denied FAPE
  2. **Reimbursement:** If private placement was appropriate and FAPE denied
  3. **Tuition reimbursement:** Even for unilateral placement (reimbursement may be reduced if parent didn't give proper notice)
- **Key requirements for reimbursement:**
  - Parent must have given written notice before removing child
  - Private placement must be appropriate
  - School must have denied FAPE
- **Note:** Endrew F. focused on prospective IEP adequacy, but courts still award compensatory relief for past denials

## COMPLEX QUESTION 7: Section 504 vs IDEA Intersection

**Question:** A student has ADHD but is passing grades with accommodations. The parent wants an IEP. What determines which is appropriate?

**My Answer:**
- **IEP (IDEA):** Requires specially designed instruction - meaning the HOW of instruction must change
- **504 Plan:** Requires related aids and services - the student can access general curriculum with accommodations
- **Decision framework:**
  - If passing with accommodations → 504 likely sufficient
  - If failing despite accommodations → need to evaluate for IEP
  - If instruction methodology needs to change → IEP needed
- **Key test:** Does the student need change to WHAT they learn (modifications) or just HOW they access (accommodations)?
- **Advocacy tip:** Document when accommodations are insufficient, grades drop, or student struggles despite support

## COMPLEX QUESTION 8: Parent Revocation of Consent

**Question:** A parent agrees to special education services but later decides they want to withdraw their child from special education. What happens?

**My Answer:**
- **Parent can revoke consent at any time** (per 2004 IDEA amendments)
- If parent revokes:
  - School MUST stop providing special education services
  - School must provide prior written notice at least 5 days before stopping services (Texas)
  - Child may be entitled to Section 504 services if they have a disability
- **Cannot be forced** to continue services
- **BUT:** Once revoked, school is no longer obligated to provide FAPE
- If parent wants services again, must go through referral/evaluation process
- **Stay-put does NOT apply** to parent-initiated withdrawal (only due process appeals)
