### 3b. What Facts Support the Allegations?

**Background:**
Austin Lerma is an 8th-grade student eligible for special education services under the disability category of Other Health Impairment (ADHD). The student's ARD committee met on December 2, 2025, and developed an IEP with measurable annual goals requiring progress monitoring and reporting concurrent with report cards using data collection, teacher reports, and observations.

**DAEP Placements:**
- First DAEP Placement: On or about September 30, 2025 through November 20, 2025
- Second DAEP Placement: On or about February 19, 2026 — **currently still placed in DAEP as of this complaint**

**First MDR (September 29, 2025):**
- ARD committee conducted manifestation determination for first DAEP placement
- Parent (Dayna Herrera) **did NOT agree** with the committee's determination
- Parent stated she believes Austin's behaviors are a manifestation of his disability and her doctor agrees
- Parent requested updated testing to evaluate eligibility for autism
- Case manager claimed all services and accommodations were being followed — however, this was before social skills were discontinued in January 2026
- Committee determined behaviors were NOT manifestation of disability — parent disputed this

**Alleged Violations and Supporting Facts:**

**Violation 1 — Failure to Implement IEP:**

The district failed to implement the IEP by not monitoring progress during DAEP placement. Specifically:

- District documentation states: "Conditions at DAEP do not allow for proper monitoring of this goal's progress"
- The teacher at Austin's home campus (North Belton Middle School) claimed they could no longer provide progress monitoring because he was moved to DAEP and they were "unable to see his grades"
- **This excuse is invalid because Austin's IEP goals were NOT based on grades** — they were behavioral and academic goals that should have been monitored through the DBRC (Daily Behavior Report Card), data collection, and teacher observations
- **No data collection occurred for ANY goal during DAEP placement** — the lack of data collection on any goal demonstrates that NONE of Austin's IEP services were implemented with fidelity as stated in his IEP
- On February 17, 2026, during a Manifestation Determination Review (MDR), the district stated Austin's IEP would be implemented during his DAEP placement — however, the district failed to fulfill this commitment

**Violation 2 — Failure to Provide Measurable, Data-Based Progress Reporting:**

The district failed to provide measurable, data-based progress reporting concurrent with report cards:

- Progress reports include vague entries such as "see note"
- No consistent data collection system was documented or provided
- Reports rely on narrative rather than objective measurement
- Academic performance reported as "1/4" and "2/4" opportunities, yet marked as "sufficient progress"
- Behavioral performance at approximately 20% toward a 90% goal, yet marked as "sufficient progress"
- **District did not provide raw data collection sheets to DAEP staff**, making data-based reporting impossible

**Violation 3 — Inaccurate and Misleading Progress Reporting:**

The district provided inaccurate and inconsistent progress reporting:

- Goals marked "sufficient progress" even when performance was significantly below goal criteria
- Conflicting documentation: A math goal marked as "Mastered" in one progress report, yet the same goal reported as "still in progress" in another version of the same report (March 13, 2026)
- Parents were given contradictory information about their son's progress

**Violation 4 — Failure to Ensure Consistent Progress Monitoring Across Settings:**

The district failed to ensure consistent progress monitoring:

- Reporting methods vary across documents (codes vs. narrative)
- No consistent system for tracking progress across time or settings
- District admits monitoring cannot occur in DAEP due to lack of data access
- Behavioral data (DBRC) was not accessible due to DAEP placement
- **The complete lack of data collection on ANY goal demonstrates that NONE of Austin's IEP services were implemented with fidelity** — this is a direct violation of the IEP requirement that services be provided as written

**ADDITIONAL VIOLATION — Discontinuation of Social Skills Services:**

The district discontinued Austin's social skills services at the start of the second semester (January 2026) when he was moved to a general education "Bronco Time" for ELA acceleration. During the MDR on February 17, 2026, the ARD committee reviewed service logs which revealed that while social skills were provided through December 2025, they were discontinued for the second semester. The parent advocate noted that the incident in question may have been directly related to this failure to implement social skills services. The district acknowledged this discontinuation but failed to provide compensatory services to replace the lost social skills support.

**Violation 5 — Denial of Parent Meaningful Participation:**

The district's failures denied the parent meaningful participation:

- Parent was properly invited to ARD meetings and MDR (procedurally correct)
- However, parent was unable to make informed educational decisions because:
  - Progress reports were inaccurate (goals "Mastered" then "in progress" for same period)
  - Progress reports were misleading (20% performance on 90% goal = "sufficient")
  - Progress reports were inconsistent across documents
- Without accurate, data-based progress information, parent could not meaningfully participate in:
  - Evaluating whether goals were being met
  - Deciding whether IEP changes were needed
  - Advocating for additional services or supports
  - Determining if compensatory services were warranted

---

**Violation 6 — Failure to Maintain Required Social Skills Services:**

The district failed to maintain Austin's social skills services as required by the IEP:

- Austin's IEP included social skills services
- These services were discontinued at the start of second semester (January 2026) when Austin was moved to general education "Bronco Time" for ELA acceleration
- Service logs reviewed during the MDR on February 17, 2026, confirmed social skills were only provided through December 2025
- The parent advocate specifically noted during the MDR that the incident "may have been directly related to this failure to implement social skills"
- The district failed to provide alternative services or compensatory supports when discontinuing social skills

This discontinuation of required IEP services directly contributed to Austin's inability to receive the social-emotional supports necessary to handle the threatening situation he faced.

---

**Violation 7 — District's Own Evidence Shows Emotional Disability Impact:**

The district's Full Individual Evaluation (FIE) dated February 5, 2026, and reviewed during the MDR, shows:

- BASC-3 (Behavior Assessment System for Children) scores show **clinically significant anger and emotional self-control** issues
- Austin now has **dual eligibility**: Other Health Impairment (ADHD) AND Emotional Disability
- The parent advocate specifically raised the issue of these clinically significant BASC scores during the MDR
- The district's own school psychologist acknowledged these scores but stated "low scores in this area do not necessarily show a direct and substantial relationship to Austin's disability"

The district cannot simultaneously acknowledge clinically significant emotional/behavioral needs in evaluation data while denying that those same needs relate to the student's eligibility. This represents a substantive violation of proper eligibility determination and denies the student the supports his documented needs require.

---

**Violation 8 — Denial of FAPE:**

Collectively, the district's failures resulted in denial of FAPE:

- IEP was not implemented during DAEP placement
- Progress was not monitored or measurable
- Reporting was inconsistent and misleading
- Staff lacked access to required data to implement goals
- Parent was denied accurate information needed to participate in decision-making

### 3d. Proposal for Resolution

To resolve these allegations, the complainant requests that the Texas Education Agency require the district to:

1. **ARD Reconvening:** Reconvene the ARD committee to review IEP implementation, evaluate progress to date, and revise the IEP to ensure goals can be monitored in all settings, including DAEP and any future alternative placements.

2. **Corrected Progress Reports:** Provide corrected progress reports that include objective, data-based measures and accurately reflect Austin's performance toward IEP goals.

3. **Consistent Monitoring System:** Develop and implement a system ensuring consistent progress monitoring across all placements, including providing DAEP staff with necessary data collection tools, access to academic and behavioral data systems, and training on progress monitoring procedures.

4. **Compensatory Services:** Provide compensatory services for any period in which the IEP was not properly implemented or progress was not monitored, including but not limited to:
   - Extended School Year (ESY) services
   - Additional instructional support
   - Related services as determined by the ARD committee

5. **Staff Training:** Provide comprehensive staff training on IEP implementation in alternative education settings, including DAEP environments, with specific focus on progress monitoring in settings without direct gradebook access.

6. **Independent Educational Evaluation (IEE):** If the district cannot demonstrate appropriate progress monitoring occurred, authorize an Independent Educational Evaluation at district expense to assess Austin's current educational needs.

7. **Future IEP Monitoring Protocol:** Establish a monitoring protocol requiring:
   - Weekly progress data collection regardless of placement
   - Monthly ARD review of progress in alternative settings
   - Parent notification within 48 hours of any inability to implement IEP services

8. **Restoration of Educational Position:** Take all steps necessary to restore Austin to the educational position he would have been in had the IEP been properly implemented, including:
   - Credit recovery if applicable
   - Goal mastery assessment
   - Service accrual verification
   - Determination of whether compensatory education is warranted for periods of denied FAPE

9. **Documentation of Compliance:** Provide TEA with documentation of compliance with all IDEA and Texas education law requirements, including proof that progress monitoring can occur in all placement settings.
