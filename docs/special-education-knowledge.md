# Special Education Knowledge Base

## FAPE (Free Appropriate Public Education)

**Definition:** Special education and related services that:
1. Are provided at public expense, under public supervision, and without charge
2. Meet the standards of the State Education Agency (SEA), including IDEA requirements
3. Include appropriate preschool, elementary, or secondary school education
4. Are provided in conformity with an IEP meeting federal requirements

*34 CFR §300.17*

---

## The Two-Prong FAPE Test

Used to determine if a school provided FAPE:

### 1. Procedural Prong
Did the school district comply with the procedures outlined in IDEA?
- Proper assessments
- Parent participation
- Required meetings and notices
- IEP team composition

### 2. Substantive Prong
Was the IEP reasonably calculated to enable the child to receive educational benefits?

Following *Endrew F. v. Douglas County School District* (2017), this means the program must be **"reasonably calculated to enable progress that is 'appropriately ambitious in light of [the child's] circumstances.'"**

---

## Rowley v. Board of Education (1982) - Original FAPE Standard

The original Supreme Court case establishing the FAPE analysis. *Rowley* set the two-prong test but was later superseded on the substantive prong by *Endrew F.* (2017).

### Rowley's Two Questions:
1. **Procedural:** Did the school follow IDEA procedures?
2. **Substantive (OLD):** Is the IEP "reasonably calculated to enable the child to receive educational benefits"?

### Rowley's Standard (before Endrew F):
- IEP must enable child to **"achieve passing marks and advance from grade to grade"**
- Did NOT require states to "maximize the potential of each handicapped child"
- Focused on **meaningful access** to education, not optimal outcomes

### What Rowley Rejected:
- "Full potential" standard
- Strict equality with non-disabled students  
- Courts dictating educational methods to states

> "The Act does not require a State to maximize the potential of each handicapped child commensurate with the opportunity provided nonhandicapped children."

---

## Endrew F. v. Douglas County School District (2017)

**Holding:** A school must offer an IEP "reasonably calculated to enable a child to make progress appropriate in light of the child's circumstances."

### Key Principles

- **Progress, not just benefit:** IEPs must aim for meaningful progress
- **Appropriately ambitious:** Every child should have chance to meet challenging objectives
- **Child-specific:** Adequacy turns on unique circumstances of the child
- **Individualization:** No "one-size-fits-all" approach

### For Different Students

- **Regular classroom:** IEP should enable achieving passing marks and advancing grade to grade
- **Not fully integrated/not grade-level capable:** Must still be "appropriately ambitious in light of his circumstances"

### What Was Rejected

- **"Merely more than de minimis"** standard (the old low bar)
- The idea that barely more than trivial progress satisfies FAPE
- "Substantially equal opportunities" argument (not required)

### Important Quotes

> "When all is said and done, a student offered an educational program providing 'merely more than de minimis' progress from year to year can hardly be said to have been offered an education at all."

> "The adequacy of a given IEP turns on the unique circumstances of the child for whom it was created."

> "The goals may differ, but every child should have the chance to meet challenging objectives."

---

## IEP Team Obligations Under Endrew F.

IEP teams must:
1. Develop IEPs that are appropriately individualized and ambitious
2. Carefully consider each child's present levels, functional performance, disability, and growth potential
3. Monitor and revise IEPs as necessary
4. If child is not making expected progress, **must revisit the IEP** and revise as needed

## Personnel Qualifications Under IDEA (OSEP 22-01)

Per 34 CFR §300.156, SEAs must ensure personnel are appropriately prepared.

### Special Education Teachers
Must have:
- Full State certification as special education teacher, OR
- Passed State licensing exam + holds license as special education teacher
- At least a bachelor's degree

### Alternate Route Requirements
Alternate route programs must include:
1. High-quality professional development (sustained, intensive, classroom-focused)
2. Intensive supervision/mentoring
3. Teaching role limited to max 3 years
4. Demonstrate satisfactory progress toward full certification

### What Cannot Be Waived
- ❌ Emergency, temporary, or provisional certification for special ed teachers
- ❌ Emergency, temporary, or provisional licensure for related services personnel

### Paraprofessionals
- Can assist if appropriately trained and supervised per State law/policy

## DOE Guidance on Endrew F. (December 2017)

The Department of Education issued Q&A clarifying *Endrew F.* implementation.

### "Reasonably Calculated" Means:
- A **prospective judgment** by the IEP Team
- Informed by: school expertise, child's progress, growth potential, parent views
- Consider: past services effectiveness, academic growth rate, behaviors interfering, parent input

### "Progress Appropriate in Light of Child's Circumstances" Means:
- Meaningful opportunities for academic/functional advancement
- Expectations appropriate for child's unique situation
- IEP must aim for the child to make progress

### If Child Not Making Expected Progress:
- IDEA requires IEP be revisited if expected progress isn't occurring
- **Must revise IEP** to address lack of progress
- Ensure child receiving appropriate interventions, services, supplementary aids
- Goals must remain individualized and ambitious

### IEP Teams Must Now:
1. Identify present levels accurately
2. Set measurable annual goals (academic + functional)
3. Measure and report progress toward goals
4. Demonstrate program is reasonably calculated for appropriate progress

### Parents' Due Process Rights:
- Unchanged by *Endrew F.*
- Parents can still opt for private placement if they believe FAPE denied
- May recover costs if: FAPE not offered timely, private placement appropriate, proper notice given

## IEP Guide (DOE, July 2000)

A comprehensive guide to the Individualized Education Program.

### The IEP Process (Step by Step)

1. **Child Find** – State identifies/locates/evaluates children with disabilities; parents can initiate
2. **Evaluation** – Assess all areas related to suspected disability; parent consent required
3. **Eligibility** – Team decides if child meets disability definition; parent can request hearing
4. **Found eligible** – IEP team meets within 30 days; parental consent needed for services
5. **IEP meeting scheduled** – Parents notified in advance, can bring others
6. **IEP written** – Team collaborates; parents part of team
7. **Services provided** – School implements as written; parents get copy
8. **IEP reviewed** – At least annually; parents can request more frequent
9. **Progress measured** – Reported to parents as often as non-disabled children
10. **Reevaluated** – At least every 3 years

### IEP Team Members
- **Parents** – Know child best, bring strengths/needs/ideas
- **Regular education teacher** – If child may participate in regular ed
- **Special education teacher** – Expertise in disability education
- **Individual who interprets evaluations** – Explains instructional implications
- **School system rep** – Has authority to commit resources
- **Additional individuals** – Advocates, professionals, etc.
- **The student** – Must be invited if transition services discussed

### Required IEP Contents
- **Current performance** – Present levels, how disability affects curriculum involvement
- **Annual goals** – Measurable, with short-term objectives
- **Special education & related services** – List of supports, modifications
- **LRE** – Extent of participation with non-disabled peers
- **State/district tests** – Any needed modifications
- **Dates/places** – When services begin, frequency, location
- **Transition services** – Age 14 (courses), age 16 (services needed)
- **Age of majority** – At least 1 year before rights transfer to student
- **Measuring progress** – How and when parents are informed

### Special Factors to Consider
- Behavior impeding learning
- Limited English proficiency
- Blindness/visual impairment (Braille)
- Communication needs
- Deafness/hard of hearing
- Assistive technology needs

### Placement (LRE)
- Educate with non-disabled peers to maximum extent appropriate
- Removal only if can't satisfy in regular class with aids/services
- Continuum: regular class → special class → special school → home → hospital → institution

### If Parents Disagree
1. Try to reach agreement with school
2. **Mediation** – Neutral third party helps
3. **Due process** – Impartial hearing officer decides
4. **Complaint to SEA** – Must resolve within 60 days

## Texas LRE Q&A (2017)

Texas-specific guidance on Least Restrictive Environment.

### Core LRE Requirements
- Students must be served with non-disabled peers to **"maximum extent appropriate"**
- Can only remove from general education if **nature/severity of disability** prevents education there, even with supplementary aids
- Placement reviewed **annually** by ARD committee

### "Maximum Extent Appropriate"
- ARD committee starts with general education as first consideration
- All placement alternatives must be considered
- More restrictive only if less restrictive **cannot be achieved satisfactorily**

### Continuum of Placements
- Regular class → Special classes → Special schools → Home instruction → Hospitals → Institutions
- Texas also includes: mainstream, homebound, hospital class, speech therapy, resource room, self-contained, off campus, nonpublic day school, vocational adjustment, residential treatment

### Supplementary Aids & Services
- Aids, services, supports provided in general education to enable inclusion
- Examples: PD for teachers, behavior consultation, co-teaching, interpreters, aides, accommodations

### Modifications vs. Accommodations
- **Accommodations:** Change *how* student learns (no change in learning expectations)
- **Modifications:** Change *what* student is expected to master

### Placement Decision Rules
- ❌ **Cannot consider:** Disability category, severity, delivery system config, service availability, space, administrative convenience
- ❌ **Need not fail** in general ed before more restrictive placement
- ❌ **Cannot remove** solely due to difficulty providing modifications
- Must show evidence of efforts to provide supplementary aids before removal

### Home Campus
- Unless IEP requires otherwise, student educated on home campus
- Removal requires justification + evidence of efforts on home campus

## Texas Co-Teaching Guidelines

Guidelines for delivering special education through co-teaching models.

### What is Co-Teaching?
- Two certified teachers collaborating in general ed classroom
- NOT: one teacher instructing while aide observes, or parallel separate instruction
- One option within continuum of services (not mandatory)

### Six Co-Teaching Approaches
1. **Station Teaching** – Content divided, students rotate
2. **Parallel Teaching** – Class split, both teachers
3. **Alternative Teaching** – One large group, one small group
4. **Teaming** – Both lead instruction
5. **One Teach, One Observe** – One instructs, one collects data
6. **One Teach, One Assist** – One leads, one supports

### Key Concepts
- **Specially Designed Instruction (SDI):** Adapting content/methodology to meet unique needs
- **Curriculum Access:** Students must access/general curriculum (TEKS in TX)
- **Inclusion:** Belief system welcoming all learners
- **UDL:** Universal Design for Learning

### Requirements
- ARD determines placement + services
- Grading determined by ARD (not teacher alone)
- Professional development required if teacher lacks IEP skills

## Texas Procedural Safeguards (January 2025)

Texas Education Agency's required notice of parent rights under IDEA.

### When Parents Must Receive Notice
- At least once per school year
- Upon initial referral or request for evaluation
- Upon first special education complaint
- Upon first due process hearing complaint
- When disciplinary change of placement
- Upon parental request

### Who Can Be "Parent" Under IDEA
- Biological/adoptive parent
- Foster parent (with training)
- Guardian
- Grandparent/stepparent/other relative
- Surrogate parent (appointed if no parent available)

### Foster Parent as Parent
- Can serve as parent if agree to participate in decisions
- Must complete training within 90 days
- School must give written notice within 7 days if refusing appointment
- Can file complaint with TEA

### Surrogate Parent
- Appointed when: no parent found, foster parent unavailable, child ward of state, or homeless youth
- Cannot be: TEA/school/agency employee, or have conflicting interests
- Must: visit child/school, review records, attend ARD, complete training

### Child Find
- State must identify, locate, evaluate ALL children with disabilities
- Includes homeless, wards of state, private school children

### Prior Written Notice (PWN)
- School must give notice BEFORE proposing or refusing any change
- Texas: **5 school days notice** required (unless parent agrees to shorter)
- Must include: action description, reasons, evaluation info, rights info, contact info, alternatives considered

### Parental Consent Required For
- Initial evaluation
- Initial provision of special education services
- Reevaluation
- Consent can be revoked; school must then provide PWN 5 days before stopping services

### Discipline Procedures
- Can remove for up to **10 school days** (not a change of placement)
- After 10 days: must provide FAPE services
- **Manifestation Determination** within 10 days of disciplinary removal
- If manifestation: must return to prior placement, conduct FBA, review BIP
- **Special Circumstances** (45 days IAES): weapon, drugs, serious bodily injury
- **Stay-Put**: Child remains in current placement during proceedings

### Dispute Resolution Options
1. **IEP Facilitation** – Independent facilitator for ARD meetings
2. **Mediation** – Voluntary, confidential, results in binding agreement
3. **State Complaint** – File with TEA, resolved within 60 days
4. **Due Process Hearing** – Impartial officer, appeal to court within 90 days
5. **Stay-Put** – Child stays in current placement during dispute

### Contact
- **SPEDTex:** 1.855.773.3839 | www.spedtex.org
- **Relay Texas:** 7-1-1 (for deaf/hard of hearing)

## Texas Paraprofessionals Guide

Working with paraprofessionals in Texas schools.

### Three Levels of Certification (19 TAC §230.61)
- **Education Aide 1:** Routine tasks under direction/supervision of certified teacher
- **Education Aide 2:** General supervision, participates in planning
- **Education Aide 3:** Most responsibility, implements methodology, works with individuals/groups

### What Paraprofessionals CANNOT Do
- ❌ Develop lesson plans
- ❌ Introduce new material/content
- ❌ Provide direct teaching portion of lesson
- ❌ Select materials for lesson
- ❌ Assign final grades
- ❌ Determine/report student progress independently
- ❌ Develop IEP goals/objectives
- ❌ Design classroom management system

### Key Principles
- Teacher is ALWAYS primary instructor
- Paraprofessional supports, doesn't replace teacher
- Goal: promote student independence
- 1:1 aide should NOT isolate student from peers
- Must have access to relevant IEP sections
- Supervised by certified special educator

## Texas Developmental Delay (DD) Eligibility (2024)

TEA's quick guide for identifying children ages 3-9 with developmental delays.

### Age Range
- **Age 3 through 9** (can maintain until re-evaluation before age 10)
- After that, must have specific disability category

### Evaluation Requirements
- **Multidisciplinary Team (MDT)** must conduct evaluation
- Must include: LSSP, educational diagnostician, or appropriately certified professional
- Use variety of assessment tools (not single measure)
- Assess in child's native language/mode of communication
- Must be comprehensive to identify ALL special education needs

### Three Criteria Options

**Criteria A:** 2 SD below mean (or 2nd percentile) in ONE area + convergent evidence
- Areas: Physical, Cognitive, Communication, Social/emotional, Adaptive

**Criteria B:** 1.5 SD below mean (or 7th percentile) in TWO+ areas + convergent evidence

**Criteria C:** Body of evidence from multiple sources showing pattern of atypical development significantly impeding progress across settings

### Documentation Required
- How DD adversely affects involvement in general curriculum
- Must be in PLAAFP statement in IEP
- Note suspected but unconfirmed disabilities

### ARD Committee Role
- Determines if child meets DD criteria
- Determines need for special education
- Must rule out other confirmed disabilities first
- Can combine DD with another disability only if other is clearly confirmed but another suspected

## Section 504: Hidden Disabilities

DOE guidance on civil rights protections for students with hidden disabilities.

### Definition of Disability (Section 504)
Has a physical/mental impairment that substantially limits one or more major life activities, OR has a record of such impairment, OR is regarded as having such impairment.

### Major Life Activities
Caring for self, manual tasks, walking, seeing, hearing, speaking, breathing, learning, working

### Hidden Disabilities (Not Readily Apparent)
- Specific learning disabilities
- Diabetes, epilepsy, allergies
- Low vision, poor hearing
- Heart disease, chronic illness
- Mental/emotional illness

### School Responsibilities Under Section 504
1. **Identify & Locate** – Annually identify unserved children
2. **FAPE** – Provide regular/special education + related aids/services
3. **LRE** – Educate with non-disabled peers to max extent appropriate
4. **Nondiscriminatory Evaluation** – Avoid misclassification
5. **Procedural Safeguards** – Parents can participate in decisions
6. **Equal Opportunity** – Nonacademic/extracurricular activities

### FAPE Under Section 504
- Regular or special education + related aids/services
- Meet individual needs **as adequately as non-disabled students**
- Different from IDEA – no IEP required

### accommodations Examples
- Medical conditions: schedule adjustments, rest time
- Learning disabilities: modified testing, notetakers
- Chronic illness: parking, time between classes
- Emotional/mental illness: adjusted schedule for counseling
- Epilepsy: accommodations for stress/triggers

## OCR Guidance: Students with Disabilities in Extracurricular Athletics (2013)

DOE Office for Civil Rights Dear Colleague Letter on equal opportunity in athletics.

### Key Principles
- Students with disabilities have right under Section 504 to equal opportunity to participate in extracurricular athletics
- Schools must NOT exclude based on stereotypes/assumptions
- Must make individualized inquiry about reasonable modifications

### What Schools Must Do
- Provide equal opportunity in existing programs
- Make reasonable modifications, aids, services (unless fundamental alteration or undue burden)
- Assess each student's needs individually

### What Schools DON'T Have to Do
- Change essential elements of the sport
- Give unfair advantage
- Compromise safety
- Create separate parallel programs (urged but not required)
- Fundamentally alter the program

### Examples of Required Modifications
- Visual starter signal for deaf runner
- Administer insulin for diabetic student in after-school club
- One-hand touch finish in swimming (if not unfair advantage)

### Examples of Violations
- Preventing student with learning disability from playing based on assumptions
- Refusing visual start cue for hearing-impaired student
- Not allowing medication administration for diabetes

### OCR Compliance Evaluation
- Individualized inquiry (not stereotypes)
- Reasonable modifications analysis
- Integrated setting to max extent appropriate
- Equal support for separate opportunities
- Section 504 FAPE includes extracurricular participation

## Texas Dyslexia Handbook (2024 Update)

TEA's official procedures for dyslexia identification and services.

### Definition of Dyslexia (TEC §38.003)
"Difficulty in learning to read, write, or spell, despite conventional instruction, adequate intelligence, and sociocultural opportunity."

Related disorders: developmental auditory imperception, dysphasia, specific developmental dyslexia, developmental dysgraphia, developmental spelling disability

### Screening Requirements
- **K-1 Universal Screening:** Required for ALL kindergarten (end of year) and 1st grade (by Jan 31)
- Beyond 1st grade: screen as appropriate
- Administered by: certified dyslexia teacher, or K-1 classroom teacher with valid certification
- NOT a formal evaluation — screening identifies "at risk"

### Screening Instruments
Must assess (K): Letter sounds, phonological awareness, word reading
Must assess (1st): Phonological awareness

### Evaluation (FIIE)
- If "at risk" persists despite adequate instruction → referral for Full Individual and Initial Evaluation (FIIE)
- Timeline: 45 school days after parental consent
- ARD committee reviews and determines eligibility

### Identification
- No single definitive test — based on multiple data points
- Screening → "at risk" → FIIE → ID (if qualified)
- Must rule out: lack of instruction, socio-economic factors, cultural differences

### Key Texas Laws
- **TEC §38.003** — Dyslexia screening/testing mandate
- **19 TAC §74.28** — State board rules
- **HB 3928 (88th Legislature)** — Latest changes to evaluation/instruction
- **TEC §28.0062** — K-3 phonics curriculum requirement

### IDEA/Section 504 Connection
- **IDEA:** Students with dyslexia may qualify under Specific Learning Disability (SLD)
- **FIIE must follow IDEA procedures**
- **Section 504:** Provides accommodations for students who don't qualify for IDEA

### Dyslexia Instruction
- Must be evidence-based, systematic, explicit phonics
- K-3 teachers: must attend literacy achievement academy
- Cannot use "three-cueing" methods

## Texas SLD Evaluation Guidance (August 2025)

TEA's comprehensive guide for evaluating Specific Learning Disabilities.

### Definition of SLD
A disorder in basic psychological processes (understanding/using language):
- Listening, thinking, speaking, reading, writing, spelling, math
- Includes: perceptual disabilities, brain injury, dyslexia, developmental aphasia

### What SLD Does NOT Include (Exclusionary Factors)
- Visual, hearing, or motor disabilities
- Intellectual disability
- Emotional disability
- Environmental, cultural, economic disadvantage
- Language proficiency issues (emergent bilinguals)

### Evaluation Process (FIIE)
- First step: **REED** (Review of Existing Evaluation Data)
- Use variety of assessment tools (NOT single measure)
- Must be nondiscriminatory, in native language, technically sound
- Comprehensively identify ALL special education needs

### Multidisciplinary Team (MDT) Requirements
- Must include: parents, general education teacher, qualified examiner
- **For dyslexia/dysgraphia:** Must include professional with specific knowledge of dyslexia, reading process, dyslexia instruction
- Must hold: dyslexia therapist license OR advanced certification

### Two Methods for SLD Identification
1. **RTI Method** — Response to scientific, research-based intervention
2. **PSW Method** — Pattern of Strengths and Weaknesses

### Eight Areas of Inadequate Achievement
- Basic reading skills
- Reading fluency
- Reading comprehension
- Written expression
- Math calculation
- Math reasoning
- Oral expression
- Listening comprehension

### Key Evaluation Requirements
- Multiple measures — NOT single test/cutoff score
- Balanced data: informal + curriculum-based + norm-referenced
- Document exclusionary factors first
- No predetermined score profiles

### Dyslexia/Dysgraphia Connection
- **Dyslexia = SLD** under IDEA (affects basic reading, reading fluency)
- **Dysgraphia** = underachievement in written expression

### Documentation
- MDT members must certify in writing
- Report to parents 5 school days before ARD meeting
- Must include impact statements, need statements, recommendations

## Endrew F. Case Law Analysis (Zirkel, 2026)

Academic analysis of Endrew F. judicial impact since 2017.

### Key Findings
- **Negligible impact** on substantive FAPE rulings
- **79% of rulings favor school districts** vs 21% for parents
- No significant change before/after Endrew F.
- Courts give cursory treatment to Endrew F. dicta

### Case Law Trends
- Consistent pro-district outcomes remain unchanged
- Limited application of: "appropriately ambitious goals," "challenging objectives"
- Some circuits use pre-existing "meaningful benefit" standard instead
- Courts recite "reasonably calculated" but don't develop nuances

### Conclusions
- Endrew F. did NOT dramatically raise the bar in practice
- "Appropriate" remains flexible/subject to interpretation
- Split between "some" vs "meaningful" benefit not resolved
- Future research: need pre-Endrew F. comparison to confirm difference

## OSEP Memo: RTI Cannot Delay Evaluations (2011)

OSEP 11-07 — Clear guidance on RTI and IDEA evaluations.

### What is RTI?
- Multi-tiered instructional framework (MTSS)
- Core characteristics:
  - High-quality research-based general ed instruction
  - Continuous progress monitoring
  - Universal screening for academic/behavioral problems
  - Multiple tiers of increasingly intense instruction

### RTI and IDEA Relationship
- RTI is FOR identification/early intervention, NOT a substitute for evaluation
- Can be used AS COMPONENT of SLD evaluation
- NOT as reason to delay/deny evaluation

### What LEAs CANNOT Do
- ❌ Use RTI to delay initial evaluation
- ❌ Use RTI to deny evaluation
- ❌ Reject referral because child hasn't done RTI

### Key Requirements
- **Child Find:** Timely identification, no delays
- **Parent right:** Request evaluation at ANY time
- **60 days:** Evaluation timeline after parental consent
- **If LEA denies eval:** Must provide written notice with reasons
- **Parent options:** Due process hearing or state complaint

## DOE Dear Colleague: IEP Alignment with State Standards (2015)

Guidance on aligning IEPs with grade-level academic content standards.

### Key Requirement
- **IEPs must be aligned with State academic content standards** for the grade in which child is enrolled
- IEP goals must be aligned with grade-level standards for ALL children with disabilities

### Definition of General Education Curriculum
- "The same curriculum as for nondisabled children" = based on State's academic content standards for grade enrolled
- This harmonizes IDEA + ESEA requirements

### IEP Team Considerations
- Consider: present levels, impact of disability on involvement/progress
- Must consider how specific disability impacts ability to advance appropriately
- For kids significantly below grade level: estimate growth toward grade-level standards they can achieve within the year
- Goals should be ambitious but achievable — "close the gap" without requiring grade-level proficiency in one year
- For most significant cognitive disabilities: still base on state content standards (may use alternate assessment)

### Implementation
- Annual goals aligned with state standards
- Include specialized instruction, supports, modifications
- Focus on college/career preparation

## DOE Q&A: IEPs, Evaluations, and Reevaluations (2010)

Guidance clarifying IDEA requirements.

### Evaluation Timeline
- **60 days** after parental consent to complete initial evaluation
- Does NOT require: eligibility determination, IEP meeting, or services within 60 days

### Medical Diagnosis
- No explicit requirement for doctor's diagnosis for eligibility
- Evaluations must use variety of assessment tools
- No single measure can be sole criterion
- State MAY require medical diagnosis for autism/ADHD IF: at public expense, free to parents, not sole criterion

### IEP Team Excusal
- Members can be excused from meetings IF:
  - Parent + agency agree in writing (area not being discussed), OR
  - For reps whose area IS discussed: submit written input BEFORE meeting
- No specific federal timeline for notification
- States can add requirements exceeding IDEA as long as they don't reduce parent rights

### Documentation Requirements
- Must document consideration of IEP requirements in writing
- Document how team members informed of changes (amendments without meeting)
- Parent must receive prior written notice for IEP amendments

### Consent Provisions
- Initial eval: parent consent required; if refused, agency MAY (not must) pursue via override
- Reevaluation: parent consent required; if no response + reasonable efforts documented, can proceed without consent
- Initial services: parental consent required
- Transition reps: parental consent required BEFORE each meeting

## OCR Guidance: Students with ADHD (2016)

Section 504 obligations for students with ADHD.

### Section 504 Basics
- Prohibits disability discrimination in federally-funded programs
- Provides FAPE: regular/special education + related aids/services
- Must meet individual needs **as adequately as non-disabled students**
- LRE: educate with non-disabled to max extent appropriate

### "Major Life Activities" for ADHD
Concentrating, reading, thinking, brain functions

### Child Find Obligation
- Must evaluate any student who "needs or is believed to need" services
- Must evaluate BEFORE initial placement and before any significant change in placement

### Common School Mistakes
- ❌ Not referring/identifying students
- ❌ Not evaluating in timely manner
- ❌ Inadequate evaluations
- ❌ Making decisions based on misunderstanding of ADHD
- ❌ Not distributing documentation to staff
- ❌ Considering cost/burden when selecting services
- ❌ Assuming high GPA = no disability
- ❌ Ignoring "inattentive type" ADHD (less disruptive)
- ❌ Stereotyping/generalizing

### Key Requirement: Mitigating Measures
- **Cannot consider** medication, coping strategies when determining if student has disability
- Must evaluate student's **unmitigated** state

### FAPE Under Section 504
- Based on **specific needs**, not cost
- Not based on stereotypes
- Include: regular/special ed, related services, supplementary aids
- LRE requirement

## Classroom Interventions for ADHD

William & Mary Training & Technical Assistance Center guide.

### DSM-V ADHD Types

1. **Predominantly Inattentive:**
   - Inaccurate/incomplete work
   - Difficulty attending, easily distracted
   - Difficulty following directions, losing materials
   - Poor organization

2. **Predominantly Hyperactive/Impulsive:**
   - Constant motion, fidgeting
   - Leaves seat, interrupts
   - Talks excessively, blurts out answers

3. **Combined Type:** Symptoms from both categories

### Diagnostic Criteria
- Symptoms appear before age 12
- In at least two settings
- Adverse effects on academic/social/emotional development
- Often co-existing conditions

- Team approach across multiple settings
- Preventive + intervention strategies
- Based on assessment data (strengths, needs, environment)
- Progress monitoring + strategy adjustments

- **Giving Directions:** Minimal steps at a time, have students repeat, provide written directions

- **Seating:** Away from distractions, near teacher/peers, avoid high-traffic areas

- **Prevention:** Nonverbal signals, choice as reward, timers, visual prompts, proximity control
- **Reinforcement:** Specific/frequent positive feedback, home-school reinforcement system
- **Verbal:** Praise, redirection, corrective feedback

### Home-School Communication
- Daily notes/checklists
- Email communication
- Homework websites
- **Caution:** Don't diagnose or recommend medication — that's for medical professionals

## Texas IEP Measurable Annual Goals Q&A (Feb 2025)

TEA guidance on writing IEP goals.

### Measurable Annual Goals Definition
- Enable student to participate in/make progress in general ed curriculum
- Address needs arising from disability
- What student can accomplish within 12 months
- Must be skills/knowledge that can be measured/mastered, not activities

### Four Critical Components (19 TAC §89.1055(b))
1. **Timeframe:** Time needed to master (weeks/date)
2. **Conditions:** How progress occurs (specific resources, supports)
3. **Behavior:** Observable, measurable performance
4. **Criterion:** How much/how often to demonstrate mastery

### PLAAFP Role
- Present Levels = foundation for goal development
- Identifies strengths and needs arising from disability

### Academic vs Functional Goals
- **Academic:** Aligns with grade-level TEKS, shows progress IN curriculum
- **Functional:** Non-academic, everyday skills enabling access to curriculum
- Speech goals: academic if aligned with curriculum, functional if teaching access skill
- Related service goals generally functional

### Standards-Based Goals
- Aligned to enrolled grade-level TEKS
- Goal is to close gap between present levels and grade-level expectations
- NOT the curriculum itself, but what student needs to make progress

### Key Requirements
- At least ONE measurable annual goal required
- Goals for EACH subject area with modified content
- If removed from general ed (even without modification), still need goal
- Generic goals like "70% mastery of grade-level" = NOT sufficient
- Must address specific needs from disability

### Short-Term Objectives/Benchmarks
- Required for students taking alternate assessments (STAAR Alt 2)
- At least 2 per annual goal
- Mastery of benchmarks CANNOT be criterion for annual goal mastery

### Mastery & Grading
- Mastering goal ≠ passing course
- Passing course ≠ mastering goal
- If goal unmastered + need persists → new similar goal

### Progress Reporting
- IEP must describe how progress measured + frequency
- ARD committee determines frequency
- Concurrent with report cards is common

### Transition/Postsecondary
- Postsecondary goals differ from annual goals
- Annual goals facilitate movement toward postsecondary goals

## DSM-V ADHD Diagnostic Criteria

Official diagnostic criteria for ADHD.

### Three Types (DSM-V)
1. **Combined (F90.2):** Both inattention + hyperactivity-impulsivity
2. **Predominantly Inattentive (F90.0):** Inattention only
3. **Predominantly Hyperactive/Impulsive (F90.1):** Hyperactivity-impulsivity only

### Symptom Requirements
- **6+ symptoms** from either list for 6+ months (age 17+: 5 symptoms)
- Symptoms NOT due to oppositional behavior/defiance
- Must negatively impact social/academic/occupational functioning

### Inattention Symptoms (6+)
- Careless mistakes, fails to give close attention to details
- Difficulty sustaining attention
- Seems not to listen when spoken to directly
- Does not follow through, fails to finish tasks
- Difficulty organizing tasks/activities
- Avoids/reluctant to engage in sustained mental effort
- Loses things necessary for tasks
- Easily distracted
- Forgetful in daily activities

### Hyperactivity-Impulsivity Symptoms (6+)
- Fidgets, taps, squirms
- Leaves seat when remaining seated expected
- Runs/climbs inappropriately
- Cannot play/engage quietly
- "On the go," driven by motor
- Talks excessively
- Blurts out answers before question completed
- Difficulty waiting turn
- Interrupts/intrudes on others

### Diagnostic Requirements
- **Age 12:** Symptoms present prior to age 12
- **Two+ settings:** Home, school, work, etc.
- **Impairment:** Interferes with social, academic, occupational functioning
- **Exclusion:** Not better explained by another mental disorder

### Severity Levels
- **Mild:** Few symptoms, minor impairment
- **Moderate:** Between mild and severe
- **Severe:** Many symptoms, marked impairment

### Associated Features
- Emotional dysregulation (quick to anger, easily frustrated)
- Neurocognitive deficits (working memory, planning, response inhibition)
- Often co-occurring conditions (learning disorders, anxiety, ODD, etc.)
- Lower academic achievement, peer rejection

## Functional Behavior Skills Affected by ADHD

10 domains of skills affected by ADHD for IEP development.

### 1. Self-Regulation
- Managing frustration, calming down, identifying emotions
- Using coping strategies (breathing, breaks, self-talk)
- IEP Impact: Dysregulation → work refusal, outbursts

### 2. Impulse Control
- Waiting before speaking, not blurting out
- Thinking before acting, controlling urges
- IEP Impact: Impulsive behaviors → disrupt learning

### 3. Sustained Attention
- Staying on-task, attending to instruction
- Persisting through non-preferred tasks
- IEP Impact: Lost instructional access, incomplete work

### 4. Task Initiation
- Beginning work promptly, using prompts
- Getting materials, transitioning into tasks
- IEP Impact: Missing instruction, avoidant behaviors

### 5. Executive Functioning
- Organizing materials, tracking assignments
- Time management, planning, prioritizing
- IEP Impact: Incomplete/late work, grades drop

### 6. Behavioral Flexibility
- Handling changes, managing transitions
- Accepting corrections, recovering from disappointment
- IEP Impact: Rigid responses → conflict, shutdowns

### 7. Social Interaction
- Taking turns, listening without interrupting
- Understanding social cues, managing conflict
- IEP Impact: Peer conflicts, social rejection

### 8. Functional Communication
- Asking for help appropriately, requesting breaks
- Expressing frustration verbally, clarifying directions
- IEP Impact: Explosive/avoidant behavior without skills

### 9. Sensory/Environmental Regulation
- Staying regulated in noisy environments
- Managing overstimulation, using sensory tools
- IEP Impact: Overstimulation → fight/flight, removal

### 10. Work Completion & Persistence
- Completing on time, working through difficulty
- Maintaining effort until completion
- IEP Impact: Work avoidance, dependency on prompting

### Why These Matter for IEP
- Directly affect access to general curriculum (IDEA 300.320)
- TEA requires functional needs addressed through instruction
- Under Endrew F: IEP must be "appropriately ambitious" based on child's actual needs
- When functional skills weak → academic progress cannot occur

## Prior Written Notice (PWN) - 7 Required Elements

Under IDEA (34 C.F.R. § 300.503(b))

### The 7 Elements
1. **Description of action** — What is proposed or refused
2. **Explanation** — Why the decision was made
3. **Basis** — Data/evaluations used as basis

Missing components = procedural violation of IDEA, potentially affecting parental participation or access to FAPE

## IEP Parent FAQ (21 Questions)

Essential questions every parent should know.

### Q1: What is an IEP?
Legally binding document specifying present levels, goals, services, accommodations. If it's in the IEP, school MUST implement it.

### Q2: Who qualifies for an IEP?
Two-step: (1) Has disability category, (2) Disability adversely affects educational performance and needs special education

### Q3: IEP vs Section 504
- 504 = access + accommodations
- IEP = specially designed instruction + stronger procedural safeguards
- Choose IEP if instruction needs to change, not just how received

### Q4: What does FAPE guarantee?
- Individualized instruction at no cost
- "Reasonably calculated" for progress appropriate to child's circumstances
- More than "barely getting by" — must be ambitious (Endrew F.)

### Q5: How to request evaluation
Write to principal + special education director. State: "requesting special education evaluation under IDEA." List concerns.

### Q6: Timeline
- Federal: 60 days from consent
- Texas: 45 school days for FIIE + 30 calendar days for ARD meeting

### Q7: Can RTI delay evaluation?
**No.** RTI can inform evaluation, not replace it.

### Q8: Disability categories
SLD, OHI, ASD, ED, ID, SLI, etc.
- ADHD → OHI
- Dyslexia → SLD

### Q9: Gifted but struggling?
**Yes** can qualify. Good grades don't rule out eligibility if working below potential.

### Q10: IEP team members
- Parent
- General ed teacher
- Special ed teacher
- LEA representative (authority + resources)
- Evaluator who can interpret results

### Q11: Consent rights
- Required for initial evaluation + initial services
- Can revoke consent (district then not obligated to provide FAPE)

### Q12: Can school require medication?
**No.** Cannot require medication as condition for evaluation or services.

### Q13: Comprehensive evaluation
Multiple tools: standardized tests, rating scales, observations, interviews. Must be non-discriminatory, in child's language.

### Q14: What evaluation includes
Academic skills, cognitive, language, social-emotional, behavior, executive functioning, adaptive skills, medical/sensory

### Q15: If school refuses evaluation
Must provide PWN with explanation + data used. Good grades alone don't rule out eligibility.

### Q16: Right to IEE at public expense?
**Yes.** If you disagree with district eval. District must either fund IEE or file due process to defend their eval.

### Q17: Reevaluation timeline
At least every 3 years. Can happen sooner if new concerns. Not more than once/year without agreement.

### Q18: Getting raw data
Right to inspect all education records. Ask for test protocols, standard scores, percentile ranks, progress graphs.

### Q19: IEP transfers across districts/states
New district must provide comparable services until they adopt or develop new IEP.

### Q20: Private evaluations factor in?
Team must consider but doesn't automatically control eligibility. Push for rationale if dismissed.

### Q21: IEP minimum contents
PLAAFP, measurable goals, services, supplementary aids, accommodations/modifications, assessment participation, LRE, progress measurement

- **Prenatal:** Prematurity, low birth weight, oxygen deprivation
- **Maternal smoking during pregnancy**
- **Lead exposure**
- **Brain injury (mTBI)**
- **Institutional deprivation**
- **Iron/zinc deficiency**

### NOT risk factors (excluded by meta-analysis)
- Sugar consumption
- Vaccines
- Maternal thyroid insufficiency
- Sleep restriction

### Treatment
- Evidence-based, open-access guide
- Available in English, Spanish, Chinese
- Psychosocial plan essential
- Focus on LMIC (low-middle income countries)

### For Evaluation
- Consider prematurity, low birth weight as risk factors
- Assess across lifespan
- Need multiple sources of data

## IEP Development - PLAAFP Examples

### Components of IEP
- Present Levels of Academic Achievement and Functional Performance (PLAAFP)
- Annual Goals (measurable)
- Special Education Services (type, location, frequency)
- Related Services
- Supplementary Aids/Services/Interventions
- State Assessment Participation
- Placement (LRE)
- Parent approval of services

### Compliant PLAAFP Example
> "When given a grade-level literary text, Student will identify the theme by providing text support in 4 out of 5 opportunities across 3 consecutive data points as measured by special education teacher records."

**Why it works:**
- Specific skill identified
- Conditions clearly stated
- Criteria for mastery defined
- Measures progress

### Non-Compliant PLAAFP Example
> "Student is failing reading."

**Why it fails:**
- No specific skill
- Vague language
- No measurable criteria
- Can't track progress

### Key Takeaway
PLAAFPs must be specific enough to drive IEP goals and measure progress. Generic statements like "failing" don't provide actionable information.

## Colorado Special Education Rules (1 CCR 301-8)

Colorado's Exceptional Children's Educational Act (ECEA) regulations.

### Key Definitions

**Administrative Unit (AU):** School district, BOCES, multi-district AU, charter school network/collaborative, or State Charter School Institute responsible for providing special education services.

### Colorado-Specific Residency Rules

**Administrative Unit of Residence (AUR):** Determined by where child resides day-to-day, with exceptions:

- Regional centers, mental health institutes, group homes → deemed to reside where parent/guardian resides
- Foster care → resident AU where foster home located
- Charter Schools/Schools of Choice → resident AU where parent/guardian resides
- **Out-of-Home Placement:** If child is in out-of-home placement but enrolled in school of origin, the school of origin's AU remains the resident AU regardless of placement location
- **Commissioner resolves disputes** if AUs disagree on residency

### Child Find (2.08)
- Must identify, locate, and evaluate children with disabilities ages 3-21
- Includes homeless, wards of state, private school children

### Consent Requirements (2.11)
- Parent must be fully informed in native language/mode of communication
- Consent must be in writing and describe activity
- Consent is voluntary and can be revoked at any time
- **Revocation is NOT retroactive** - records not amended to remove references to services provided before revocation

### Children with Disabilities (2.09)
Colorado defines 13 disability categories including specific criteria.

### Developmental Delay (2.09(13))
- Ages 3-8 only
- Used when clear determination cannot be made under other categories
- Requires specific scoring thresholds and multiple data sources

### LRE Requirements (2.29)
- To maximum extent appropriate, children educated with non-disabled peers
- Removal from general education only if nature/severity of disability requires it, even with supplementary aids/services

### Extended School Year (ESY)
- Child reaching age 21 during academic year has right to complete semester or attend until graduation
- **NOT entitled to ESY** during summer following that academic year

### Abbreviated School Day (2.01)
- Any day where child receives instruction for fewer hours than majority of peers
- Requires IEP Team or 504 Team approval for regular abbreviated schedule
- Both planned and unplanned abbreviated days covered

### Educational Surrogate Parent (2.15)
- Assigned when parent unknown, unavailable, or child is ward of state
- Represents child in all educational decision-making

## Montana Special Education (A.R.M. 10.16 + Parent Handbook)

Montana's special education rules under the Exceptional Children's Educational Act.

### Age Range (Montana-Specific)
- **Federal:** Ages 3-21
- **Montana:** Ages 3-20 (if student has NOT turned 20 before September 10 of new school year)
- If district serves non-disabled students 18-20, must also serve students with disabilities in that age range

### Evaluation Timeline
- **60 calendar days** from receiving written parental permission to complete evaluation
- Must have written parent consent before evaluation

### Consent Requirements
- Written parental consent required for initial evaluation
- Parent can withhold consent for special education services
- If parent refuses, district is released from obligation to provide FAPE
- Rights transfer to student at age 18

### IEP Team Members
- At least one parent/guardian
- Regular education teacher (if attending/will attend regular ed)
- Special education teacher
- Administrator qualified to supervise services
- Individual knowledgeable about general curriculum
- Individual knowledgeable about available resources
- Individual who can interpret evaluation results
- Student (when appropriate)
- Other persons with expertise about child

### IEP Required Contents
- Preferences, Interests, Strengths, Educational Concerns
- Present Level of Academic Achievement and Functional Performance (PLAAFP)
- Annual Goals
- Related Services
- Delivery of Services (start date, duration, frequency)
- Placement in LRE
- Supplemental Aids and Services (accommodations/modifications)
- Extended School Year (ESY)
- Re-evaluation (every 3 years)
- Transition plan (starting age 16, can be 14 if IEP team agrees)
- Signature lines

### Extended School Year (ESY)
- Provided beyond regular school year if IEP team determines necessary
- Standard: regression/recoupment analysis
- Factors: nature/severity of disability, parental ability to provide structure, behavioral/physical impairments, vocational needs
- At no cost to parents

### Transition Services
- **Must begin at age 16**
- Can begin at age 14 if IEP team agrees appropriate
- Student must be invited to transition IEP meeting
- Must include: measurable postsecondary goals (training, education, employment, independent living)
- Must invite other agencies (e.g., Montana Vocational Rehabilitation and Blind Services - VRBS)

### Unique Montana Provisions

**Developmental Delay (Age 3-8):**
- 2+ SD below norm in ONE area, OR
- 1.5 SD below norm in TWO+ areas (cognitive, physical, communication, social-emotional, adaptive)

**Specific Learning Disability - Two Pathways:**
1. **RTI Method:** Response to scientific, research-based intervention
2. **Severe Discrepancy:** 50%+ probability of 2 SD discrepancy between cognitive ability and achievement

**Autism Criteria:**
- At least one characteristic in each of three areas:
  - Social communication
  - Social interaction  
  - Restricted/repetitive behaviors/interests
- Total of 8+ characteristics required

**Speech-Language Impairment:**
- Articulation errors persisting 1 year beyond age when 90% of students have acquired sounds

- **Montana Empowerment Center:** 1-877-870-1190
- **OPI (Office of Public Instruction):** State standards body
- **Vocational Rehabilitation:** Pre-ETS program for transition

## Kentucky Special Education Regulations (707 KAR Chapter 1)

Kentucky's specific regulations for special education programs. These regulations are codified under Kentucky Administrative Regulations Title 707.

### 707 KAR 1:290 - Free Appropriate Public Education (FAPE)

**Scope:** Children ages 3-21, advancing from grade to grade regardless of disability.

**Key Requirements:**

- FAPE available to all children with disabilities aged 3-21 residing within district boundaries
- Even if child has not failed or been retained, FAPE provided based on unique needs
- Not required for students 18+ placed in adult correctional facility if not previously identified

**Residential Placement:** If ARC determines necessary, LEA must provide program including nonmedical care and room/board

**Program Options:** Children with disabilities must have access to same variety of educational programs available to non-disabled (art, music, career tech, etc.)

**Nonacademic Services:** Must include equal opportunity for participation in:
- Counseling services
- Athletics
- Transportation
- Health services
- Recreational activities
- Special interest groups/clubs
- Employment opportunities

**Physical Education:** Either specially designed services OR opportunity to participate in regular PE

**Assistive Technology:** Must be made available if required as part of IEP; can include home use if needed for FAPE

**Extended School Year (ESY):** Available as necessary for FAPE; determination made individually; cannot limit by disability category or unilaterally limit type/amount/duration

**Prohibition of Mandatory Medication:** Cannot require prescription for controlled substances as condition for evaluation or services

### 707 KAR 1:300 - Child Find, Evaluation, and Reevaluation

**Child Find Requirements:**

- Must locate, identify, and evaluate children ages 3-21
- Includes: private school children, migratory children, homeless children, wards of state, students advancing grade-to-grade who may have disability
- For preschool: ensure smooth transition from early intervention

**Referral System:**

- Must explain how referrals from any source will be accepted and acted upon timely
- Must prevent inappropriate over-identification or disproportionate representation
- Child must receive appropriate instruction/intervention prior to referral

**Evaluation Procedures:**

- Full and individual evaluation before providing services
- Tests must be: non-discriminatory, in child's native language
- Cannot use single procedure as sole criterion
- Must assess ALL areas related to suspected disability
- Comprehensively identify ALL special education needs

**Evaluation Timeline:**

- 60 school days from parental consent to complete evaluation
- Does NOT apply if: child moves to new LEA before completion (if making sufficient progress), or parent fails to produce child for evaluation

**Reevaluation:**

- At least every 3 years
- Can consist of review of existing data
- Not required more than once per year unless parent and LEA agree
- Must evaluate before determining child is no longer eligible

**Graduation/Exit:** Summary of academic achievement and functional performance with postsecondary recommendations required

### 707 KAR 1:310 - Determination of Eligibility

**Eligibility Determination:**

- ARC determines if child is child with disability requiring specially designed instruction
- Must provide copy of evaluation report and eligibility documentation to parent

**Exclusionary Factors (cannot be primary determinant):**

- Lack of appropriate instruction in reading (including essential components)
- Lack of appropriate instruction in math
- Limited English proficiency (unless child otherwise meets eligibility)

**Sources of Information:**

- Response to scientific, research-based interventions
- Vision, hearing, communication screenings
- Parental input
- Aptitude and achievement tests
- Teacher recommendations
- Physical condition
- Social or cultural background
- Adaptive behavior
- Behavioral observations

**SLD Evaluation (Additional Requirements):**

- ARC must include professionals relative to area of concern (school psychologist, speech-language pathologist, educational specialist)
- Team must be collectively qualified to: conduct diagnostic assessments, interpret data, develop recommendations, deliver/monitor SDI

**SLD Determination:** Child has SLD if:
- Provided appropriate learning experiences/instruction for age/grade-level standards
- Does NOT achieve adequately in one or more areas (oral expression, listening comprehension, written expression, basic reading skills, reading fluency, reading comprehension, math calculation, math reasoning)
- OR fails to achieve sufficient progress based on response to research-based intervention
- OR exhibits pattern of strengths/weaknesses relative to ability

**Exclusionary Factors for SLD:**

- Visual, hearing, or motor impairment
- Mental disability
- Emotional-behavioral disability
- Cultural factors
- Environmental or economic disadvantage
- Limited English proficiency

**Observation Requirement:** At least one team member other than regular education teacher must observe child in learning environment

**Documentation:** Must include statement of: whether SLD exists, basis for determination, relevant behaviors, relationship to academic functioning, medical findings, whether child achieves commensurate with age/ability, patterns of strengths/weaknesses, effects of exclusionary factors, instructional strategies used

### 707 KAR 1:320 - Individual Education Program (IEP)

**Timeline:**

- IEP in effect at beginning of school year
- Must be in effect BEFORE specially designed instruction provided
- Implemented as soon as possible after ARC meeting

**60 School-Day Timeline:**
- Complete evaluation within 60 school days of parental consent for initial evaluation
- ARC meeting for IEP within 30 days of eligibility determination

**ARC Membership:**

- Parents
- At least one regular education teacher (if child may participate in regular ed)
- At least one special education teacher or provider
- LEA representative (qualified to provide/supervise SDI, knowledgeable about general curriculum and LEA resources)
- Individual who can interpret evaluation results
- Individual with knowledge/special expertise regarding child (at parent or LEA discretion)
- Related services personnel as appropriate
- Child (if appropriate)

**Meeting Notice:**

- 7 days advance notice for regular meetings
- 24 hours for disciplinary change in placement or safety issue
- Must include: purpose, time, location, who will attend, notice of right to invite others, for transition: notice of student invitation and other agency representatives

**Parent Participation:**

- Parents must be afforded opportunity to participate in ALL ARC meetings
- LEA shall ensure participation via telephone calls or video conferencing if parent cannot attend
- If parent doesn't attend, LEA must document attempts to arrange mutually agreed time/place

**IEP Contents:**

- Present levels of academic achievement and functional performance (how disability affects involvement in general curriculum)
- Measurable annual goals (academic and functional) enabling progress in general curriculum
- Specially designed instruction and related services to be provided
- Extent of participation with nondisabled children
- Individual accommodations for state/districtwide assessments
- Projected date of beginning services, frequency, location, duration
- How progress toward goals will be measured and reported
- At least one year before age 18: statement that rights transfer to child
- Transition services (by age 14 for courses, age 16 for services needed)
- For child with behavior impeding learning: consider positive behavioral interventions
- For limited English proficiency: consider language needs
- For blind/visually impaired: provide for Braille instruction unless determined not appropriate
- For deaf/hard of hearing: consider language and communication needs, direct communications in child's mode
- Consider assistive technology needs

**Transition Services:**

- By 8th grade or age 14: statement of transition service needs aligned with Individual Learning Plan
- By age 16: measurable postsecondary goals based on age-appropriate transition assessments (training, education, employment, independent living) and transition services needed
- If agency fails to provide transition services, LEA must reconvene to identify alternative strategies

**IEP Accountability:**

- LEA must provide services in accordance with IEP and make good faith effort to assist child in achieving goals
- Parents retain right to request revision or invoke due process

**Transfer Students:**

- Within Kentucky: provide comparable services until new IEP adopted
- From outside Kentucky: provide comparable services until evaluation and new IEP developed
- Receiving LEA shall take reasonable steps to obtain records

**Private School Placements by LEA:**

- LEA initiates ARC meeting before placing
- Private school representative must attend
- LEA remains responsible for compliance

### 707 KAR 1:340 - Procedural Safeguards and State Complaint Procedures

**Parent Participation:**

- Right to inspect/review ALL education records
- Right to participate in ALL ARC meetings
- LEA must provide written notice of ARC meetings

**Independent Educational Evaluation (IEE):**

- Parent has right to obtain IEE
- If parent requests at public expense because they disagree with LEA's evaluation, LEA must:
  - Initiate due process hearing to show their evaluation is appropriate, OR
  - Provide IEE at public expense unless they demonstrate in due process that parent's evaluation didn't meet LEA criteria
- Criteria for IEE (location, qualifications) same as LEA's evaluation
- Parent entitled to one IEE at public expense each time LEA conducts evaluation with which parent disagrees

**ARC Meeting Notice:**

- 7 days before (except disciplinary/safety: 24 hours)
- Must indicate purpose, time, location, who will attend
- Must inform parent of right to invite individuals with knowledge/expertise
- For child turning 16: must indicate purpose of considering postsecondary goals and transition services, that student will be invited, any other agency invited

**Prior Written Notice (PWN):**

- Must be provided before LEA implements any proposal or refusal regarding identification, evaluation, placement, or provision of FAPE
- Must include:
  - Description of action proposed or refused
  - Explanation of why LEA proposes or refuses
  - Description of other options considered and reasons for rejection
  - Description of evaluation procedures, assessments, records used as basis
  - Description of other relevant factors
  - Statement of procedural safeguards
  - Sources for parents to contact for assistance

**Procedural Safeguards Notice:**

- Given to parents once per school year
- Also provided upon: initial referral/request for evaluation, first state complaint, first due process filing, disciplinary change of placement, upon parent request

**Parental Consent:**

- Required for: initial evaluation, initial provision of specially designed instruction and related services, reevaluation
- If parent refuses or fails to respond, LEA may pursue via due process but is NOT required to
- For child in state custody: may proceed without parent consent if cannot locate parent, rights terminated, or court-appointed representative gives consent

**Surrogate Parent:**

- Must appoint if: no parent can be identified, LEA cannot locate parents, child is ward of state, child is unaccompanied homeless youth
- Cannot be: employee of KDE, LEA, or any agency involved in education/care of child
- Must have no personal/professional conflict of interest
- Must have knowledge and skills to ensure adequate representation
- Must be assigned within 30 days of determination of need

**State Complaint Procedures:**

- KDE has 60 days after complaint filed to investigate
- Any organization or individual can file signed written complaint
- Complaint must include: statement of violation, facts, signature/contact info, name/residence of child, name of school, description of problem, proposed resolution, violation occurred within one year
- Either party can appeal to Commissioner within 15 business days

**Mediation:**

- Voluntary, conducted by qualified impartial mediator
- KDE maintains list of qualified mediators (not employees of KDE or LEA)
- Sessions scheduled within 60 days, location convenient to both parties
- If resolution reached: legally binding written agreement executed
- Discussions confidential and cannot be used as evidence in due process

**Dispute Resolution Meeting:**

- Within 15 days of receiving notice of due process request, LEA must convene meeting with parent
- Purpose: allow parent to discuss request, discuss facts, give LEA opportunity to resolve
- LEA representative with decision-making authority must attend
- Attorney for LEA cannot attend unless parent has attorney
- If resolution reached, agreement signed within 30 days is enforceable
- If not resolved within 30 days, due process hearing may occur

**Due Process Hearing:**

- 45-day timeline for final decision
- Parent or LEA may initiate

### 707 KAR 1:360 - Confidentiality of Information

**Parent Access Rights:**

- Right to inspect and review ALL education records
- LEA must comply without unnecessary delay, BEFORE any IEP meeting, dispute resolution meeting, or due process hearing, and no later than 45 days after request
- Right to response to reasonable requests for explanations
- Right to copies if failure to provide would prevent inspection/review
- Right to have representative inspect/review

**Record of Access:**

- LEA must keep record of all parties obtaining access (except parents and authorized employees)
- Record: name of party, date access given, purpose authorized

**Fees:**

- LEA may charge for copies if it doesn't prevent parent from exercising right to inspect/review
- Cannot charge for search or retrieval

**Amendment of Records:**

- Parent may request amendment if believed inaccurate, misleading, or violates privacy rights

- Parental consent required before disclosing personally identifiable information to anyone other than officials collecting/using information under 707 KAR Chapter 1

- Must assign staff member responsible for confidentiality
- Employees must receive training on confidentiality requirements
- Must maintain public listing of names/positions of employees who may have access

### 707 KAR 1:370 - Children with Disabilities Enrolled in Private Schools

**FAPE for Private School Students:**

- If parent places child in private school after FAPE offered, LEA not required to pay for private education
- Disagreements subject to due process procedures
- Reimbursement may be awarded if LEA did not offer FAPE in timely manner and private placement appropriate

**Reimbursement May Be Reduced/Denied If:**

- Parent did not inform LEA at last ARC meeting they were rejecting proposed placement
- Parent did not give written notice at least 10 business days prior to removal
- Parent did not make child available for evaluation (if LEA informed of intent to evaluate)
- Judicial finding of unreasonable parent actions

**Reimbursement NOT Reduced If:**

- Parent illiterate
- Compliance would likely result in physical or serious emotional harm
- School prevented parent from providing notice
- Parent did not receive notice of obligation to provide notice

**Child Find:**

- LEA must locate, identify, evaluate ALL private school children with disabilities
- Activities comparable to public school children
- Must include parentally-placed private school children residing in other states
- Child find completed in time period comparable to public school students

**Service Plan:**

- Developed for each private school child designated to receive services
- Must meet IEP requirements to extent appropriate
- Developed, reviewed, revised consistent with IEP procedures

**Proportionate Share:**

- LEA must spend proportionate amount of federal IDEA funds for parentally-placed private school children
- Determined after child find completed and child count submitted (December 1)

**Consultation:**

- LEA must consult with private school representatives and parents regarding:
  - Child find process
  - Determination of proportionate share
  - How consultation will operate throughout year
  - Types of services, how apportioned if funds insufficient
  - Written explanation if LEA disagrees with private school views

**Services:**

- Must be provided by personnel meeting same standards as public school
- Private school teachers don't have to meet "highly-qualified" requirements
- May receive different amount of services than public school children
- No individual right to services, but student shall receive services in service plan

**Location:**

- May be provided at site determined by LEA
- Transportation provided if necessary: from private school or home to service site, and from service site to private school or home
- LEA NOT required to provide transportation from home to private school

**Due Process:**

- Due process procedures do NOT apply to complaints about services plan not provided
- Due process DOES apply to: Child Find responsibilities, evaluation and eligibility for private school children
- Private school official can file state complaint if LEA did not engage in timely/meaningful consultation

### 707 KAR 1:380 - Monitoring and Recovery of Funds

**Monitoring:**

- Kentucky Department of Education (KDE) conducts regular monitoring of LEAs
- **Off-site monitoring:** Review of self-assessment, reports, complaints, due process hearings, finance reports, inclusion in assessment/accountability
- **On-site monitoring:** Record reviews, staff interviews, parent surveys, school/classroom visits

**Corrective Action Plan (CAP):**

- LEA has 30 business days to submit CAP after receiving noncompliance report
- CAP must include: statement of matter to be corrected, steps to correct and document compliance
- KDE notifies LEA of CAP status within 30 business days
- Noncompliance must be corrected within 12 months

**Causes for Imposing Sanctions:**

- Fails to comply with CAP
- Fails to comply with final decision in complaint investigation or due process hearing
- Fails to manage special education program in compliance with law
- Fails to manage funds in compliance with law
- Obtains funds through deception/falsification
- Found in noncompliance by court

**Progressive Sanctions:**

1. Intensive assistance (minimum 2 years): consultation, training, technical assistance, or special education mentor
2. Conditional approval of IDEA funds
3. Withholding of IDEA funds
4. Withholding of SEEK (Support Education Excellence in Kentucky) add-on funds
5. Other actions under state/federal law

**Child Count Audit:**

- December 1 child count subject to audit
- If count exceeds actual served or includes ineligible children, LEA must reduce count or return funds
- Audit may be triggered by: recurring noncompliance, substantiated complaints, CAP failures, unusual count data (>15% of population, no year-to-year change, high low-incidence, unusually low percentages)
- LEA has 30 business days to submit additional information to demonstrate compliance
- Follow-up audit conducted at CAP completion

### 707 KAR 1:270 - Kentucky Special Education Mentor Program

**Purpose:** Assign experienced special education professionals to assist LEAs in remedying deficiencies before imposing sanctions.

**Mentor Assignment:**

- KDE assigns mentor to assist district-wide OR school-specific deficiencies
- Obtain voluntary compliance before sanctions under 707 KAR 1:015
- Assignment completed upon verification of compliance

**Mentor Criteria:**

- Must submit letter of request and curriculum vitae
- Must be certified in area of special education

**Selection Committee:**

- 3-year terms
- Includes: State Advisory Panel rep, superintendent/principal, special ed/regular ed teachers, special ed administrator, two parents from IDEA parent project, parent advocacy organization rep
- Director of Division of Exceptional Children Services serves as chairperson
