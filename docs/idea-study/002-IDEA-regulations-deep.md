# 34 CFR Part 300 – IDEA Regulations (Deep Dive)

---

## Overview
**Part 300** is the regulatory implementation of the **Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA)**.  It is organized into **subparts** that mirror the major statutory components:

- **Subpart A – General** – purpose, applicability, definitions.
- **Subpart B – State Eligibility** – eligibility criteria, FAPE, LRE, evaluation, IEP, procedural safeguards, due‑process, private‑school placement, by‑pass, etc.
- **Subpart C – LEA Eligibility** – conditions for local agencies to receive Part B funds.
- **Subpart D – Evaluations, Eligibility, IEPs, Placements** – parental consent, evaluation procedures, specific‑learning‑disability rules, IEP development & revision.
- **Subpart E – Procedural Safeguards & Due Process** – notices, mediation, hearings, attorney‑fees, disciplinary procedures.
- **Subpart F – Monitoring, Enforcement & Confidentiality** – state monitoring, data reporting, confidentiality of records.
- **Subpart G – Grants, Allotments & Use of Funds** – Part B funding formulas, technical assistance, Indian tribal provisions.
- **Subpart H – Part C (Preschool) Grants** – early‑intervention eligibility & funding.

Each subpart is further broken into numbered sections (e.g., **§300.101‑300.112 – FAPE requirements**).  The sections below provide a concise cheat‑sheet for advocates.

---

## Subpart A – General (300.1‑300.45)
| Section | Key Point |
|---------|-----------|
| **§300.1** – *Purposes* | Guarantees a **Free Appropriate Public Education (FAPE)**, protects parent/child rights, and promotes accountability. |
| **§300.2** – *Applicability* | Applies to every State receiving Part B funds and to **all public agencies** (SEA, LEA, ESA, charter schools) that provide special education, regardless of funding source. |
| **§300.4‑300.45** – *Definitions* | Critical definitions for **disability categories (300.8)**, **FAPE (300.17)**, **LRE (300.114‑300.120)**, **related services (300.34)**, **assistive technology (300.5‑300.6)**, **parent (300.30)**, **IEP (300.22‑300.24)**, etc. |
| **§300.8** – *Child with a disability* – lists **13 statutory disability categories** (intellectual, hearing, speech‑language, visual, emotional disturbance, orthopedic, autism, traumatic brain injury, other health impairment, specific learning disability, deaf‑blind, multiple disabilities, developmental delay for ages 3‑9). |

## Subpart B – State Eligibility (300.100‑300.199)
### FAPE Requirements (300.101‑300.112)
- **§300.101‑300.102** – Define **FAPE** as education at public expense, meeting state standards, in an **IEP‑conforming** program.
- **§300.103‑300.113** – Additional obligations:
  - **§300.103** – Methods & payments (states must cover **excess costs** when required).
  - **§300.104** – **Residential placement** rules.
  - **§300.105** – **Assistive technology** provision.
  - **§300.106** – **Extended school year (ESY)** services when needed.
  - **§300.107‑300.110** – **Non‑academic services**, **physical education**, **Full Educational Opportunity Goal (FEOG)**, **program options**.
  - **§300.111** – **Child find** obligations (identify, locate, evaluate all children with disabilities).  Includes developmental‑delay child‑find for ages 3‑9.
  - **§300.112** – **IEP** must be **written, individualized, and based on evaluation data**.

### Least‑Restrictive Environment (LRE) (300.114‑300.120)
- **§300.114** – **LRE requirement** – children must be educated with nondisabled peers *to the maximum extent appropriate*.
- **§300.115** – **Continuum of placements** – from full inclusion to separate classes to specialized schools.
- **§300.116‑300.119** – **Placement criteria**, **non‑academic settings**, **private‑school placement**, **technical assistance**.
- **§300.120** – **Monitoring** – SEAs must monitor compliance with LRE.

### Evaluation Requirements (300.300‑300.317)
- **§300.300** – **Parental consent** required before evaluation (may be waived in emergencies).
- **§300.301‑300.306** – **Initial evaluation**, **screening vs. evaluation**, **reevaluation** (at least every 3 years or sooner if needed).
- **§300.307‑300.311** – **Specific learning disability (SLD) identification procedures** (criterion‑referenced, pattern analysis, response‑to‑intervention).
- **§300.312‑300.317** – **Evaluation procedures**, **reporting**, **determination of eligibility**.

### IEP Requirements (300.320‑300.324)
- **§300.320** – **Definition of IEP** – written statement of services, goals, placement, and evaluation dates.
- **§300.321** – **IEP Team composition** – parent(s), child (when appropriate), regular ed teacher, special ed teacher, related‑service provider(s), and a **qualified** representative of the LEA.
- **§300.322** – **Parent participation** – must be given notice and opportunity to participate.
- **§300.323** – **IEP effective date** – must be in effect **no later than 30 days** after adoption (or 10 days for some states).
- **§300.324‑300.328** – **IEP development, revision, and transition** – annual review, revisions as needed, and **transition planning** starting at age 16 (or earlier per state).

### Procedural Safeguards (300.500‑300.536)
- **§300.500‑300.507** – **Responsibility** of SEAs/LEAs to provide **notice of rights**, **prior written notice** of any proposed change, and **opportunity to examine records**.
- **§300.508‑300.509** – **Mediation** – optional early‑resolution method; must be offered before filing a due‑process complaint.
- **§300.510‑300.517** – **Due‑process complaint** – filing procedures, timelines, impartial hearing, **decision‑making**, **appeal**, **finality**.
- **§300.518** – **Child’s status** during proceedings (remains in placement unless changed by order).
- **§300.519‑300.520** – **Surrogate parents** and **transfer of parental rights** at age of majority.
- **§300.527‑300.529** – **Discipline procedures** – school‑initiated removal must follow IDEA safeguards.

### Due Process (300.507‑300.518) – see above under procedural safeguards.

## Subpart F – Monitoring, Enforcement & Confidentiality (300.600‑300.647)
- **§300.600‑300.609** – **State monitoring** – SEAs must develop **performance plans**, collect data, and submit **annual reports**.
- **§300.610‑300.627** – **Confidentiality of records** – defines **personally identifiable information**, **access rights**, **amendment procedures**, and **hearing rights** for privacy disputes.
- **§300.640‑300.647** – **Program‑information reporting** – annual count of children served, **disproportionality analysis**, and **certification**.

## Subpart H – Part C (Preschool) Grants (300.800‑300.818)
- Provides **early‑intervention services** for infants/toddlers with disabilities (ages 0‑3) and **preschool** services for ages 3‑5.
- Mirrors many Part B requirements (IEP/IFSP, child‑find, eligibility, transition to Part B).

## Practical Cheat‑Sheet for Advocates
1. **Know the key sections** you’ll cite most often:
   - **FAPE** – §300.101‑300.112
   - **LRE** – §300.114‑300.120
   - **IEP** – §300.320‑300.324
   - **Procedural Safeguards** – §300.500‑300.536
   - **Due Process** – §300.507‑300.518
2. **Timeline (federal baseline)**
   - **Initial evaluation** – within **30 days** of referral (state may be shorter).
   - **IEP meeting** – within **30 days** of eligibility determination.
   - **IEP revisions** – at least **annually**, or sooner if needed.
   - **Re‑evaluation** – at least **every 3 years** (or sooner if change indicated).
3. **Common violations**
   - Failure to **conduct timely evaluation** or **provide ESY**.
   - Placement **outside LRE** without proper justification.
   - **Denied parent participation** or inadequate **prior written notice**.
   - **Improper due‑process** handling (no mediation, missed timelines).
4. **Evidence checklist for due‑process**
   - Referral documentation, evaluation reports, **IEP** (all versions), **progress reports**, **communication logs**, **prior written notices**, **mediation/complaint filings**, **attendance/placement records**.
5. **Standard of review** (circuit‑specific)
   - **FAPE** – **de novo** substantive review; **LRE** – often **substantial‑evidence** standard.
   - **IEP adequacy** – **substantial‑evidence** for compliance; **procedural** violations – **arbitrary/ capricious**.
