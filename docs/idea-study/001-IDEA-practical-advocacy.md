# IDEA Study Notes - Practical Advocacy Knowledge

**Date:** 2026-03-26

## Timeline Requirements (Critical for Advocates)

### Evaluation Timelines
| Action | Timeline | Citation |
|--------|----------|----------|
| Request to district | District must respond within **15 days** | 300.300(a) |
| Complete evaluation | Must be completed within **60 days** (or state timeline) | 300.300(c) |
| Provide prior written notice | Within reasonable time before proposed change | 300.503 |

### IEP Meeting Timelines
| Action | Timeline | Citation |
|--------|----------|----------|
| IEP meeting to develop | Must occur within **30 days** of initial evaluation consent | 300.323(a) |
| Annual IEP review | At least **once per year** | 300.324(b) |
| Reevaluation | At least **every 3 years** (triennial) | 300.303(a) |

### Placement/Timeline Claims
| Issue | Timeline | Notes |
|-------|----------|-------|
| Compensatory services for missed services | No fixed federal timeline | Courts order based on "compensatory" standard |
| Manifestation determination review (MDR) | Within **10 school days** of behavior | 300.530(e) |
| Expulsion/suspension over 10 days | Must provide FAPE | 300.530(b),(c) |

## Key Court Cases (Interpretations That Matter)

### FAPE Standard
- **Board of Education v. Rowley (1982)** — "Appropriate" not "best"; basic floor of opportunity
- **Endrew F. v. Douglas County (2017)** — "Substantively appropriate" — must be meaningfully ambitious

### LRE/Placement
- **Rachel H. v. Hazard Independent Schools (4th Cir. 2023)** — Strong LRE interpretation
- **California v. Romain (9th Cir. 2023)** — Private school placement when public fails

### Procedural Violations
- **Winkelman v. Parma City School District (2007)** — Parents have right to self-representation
- **Honig v. Doe (1988)** — Cannot unilaterally expel for behaviors stemming from disability

### Compensatory Services
- **Reid ex rel. Reid v. District of Columbia (2005)** — "compensation for the denial of appropriate educational services"

## Most Common IDEA Violations (For Advocacy Focus)

### 1. Evaluation Delays
- Districts miss 60-day timeline
- Parents don't know they can request independent evaluation at district expense

### 2. IEP Deficiencies
- Goals not measurable
- Services not specific (minutes/frequency)
- Present levels don't drive goals

### 3. LRE Violations
- Automatic placement in self-contained
- Failure to try general education with aids first

### 4. Procedural Failures
- No prior written notice for changes
- Parent not given procedural safeguards copy
- Meeting times not convenient for parents

### 5. Transition Violations
- Transition services not started by age 16
- No measurable transition goals
- Student not invited to meeting

## Parent's Procedural Rights (Quick Reference)

### Must Be Given at IEP Meeting:
1. Procedural safeguards notice (once per year)
2. Prior written notice before any change
3. Opportunity to examine records
4. Right to consent/deny consent

### Dispute Resolution Options:
1. **Due Process Hearing** — Fastest, most formal
2. **State Complaint** — Slower, investigates violations
3. **Mediation** — Voluntary, non-binding
4. **Civil Action** — After due process exhausted

### Statute of Limitations:
- Due process: **2 years** from act (or should have known)
- State complaint: Must file within time state sets
- Civil action: **90 days** after due process decision
